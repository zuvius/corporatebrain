# COMPETITION ANALYSIS: Enterprise AI Knowledge Platforms
## Corporate Brain Market Intelligence Report

**Report Date:** April 2026  
**Market Segment:** Enterprise AI Knowledge Management / RAG Platforms  
**Analysis Scope:** Direct competitors offering sovereign, multi-tenant AI knowledge fabrics

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The enterprise AI knowledge management market is experiencing explosive growth, with the sector projected to reach $53 billion by 2030. This analysis identifies **11 direct and indirect competitors** to Corporate Brain's "Sovereign Context Fabric" positioning.

**Key Finding:** The market has two distinct tiers:
- **Tier 1 (Enterprise-First):** Glean, Moveworks, Hebbia - custom pricing, deep enterprise integrations
- **Tier 2 (Self-Serve/PLG):** Guru, Sana, Dashworks - transparent pricing, product-led growth

**Corporate Brain's Differentiation Opportunity:** No competitor combines true multi-tenant architecture with sovereign deployment options AND transparent pricing. This represents a clear market gap.

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS FOR CORPORATE BRAIN

### 1. Differentiation Strategy

**Position:** "The only sovereign, multi-tenant AI knowledge fabric"

Key differentiators to emphasize:
- **True Multi-tenancy** - Service provider architecture Glean/Moveworks lack
- **Sovereign Deployment** - On-premise/air-gapped options unavailable from competitors
- **Transparent Pricing** - Publish pricing while competitors hide behind sales
- **Open Architecture** - MCP support, API-first, extensible (vs. black-box solutions)

### 2. Pricing Strategy

**Recommended Model:**
- **Starter:** $299/month (up to 50 users, 100k documents, 10k queries/mo)
- **Growth:** $999/month (up to 200 users, 500k documents, 50k queries/mo)
- **Scale:** $2,999/month (up to 1000 users, 2M documents, 200k queries/mo)
- **Enterprise:** $7,999/month (unlimited users, documents, queries)
- **Service Provider:** $4,999/month + revenue share (multi-tenant, white-label)

**Metering Dimensions:**
- Document count (storage + embedding costs)
- Query volume (AI API costs)
- Data ingest volume (processing costs)
- Tenant count (multi-tenancy overhead)

**Why this works:**
- **Predictable costs** for customers - no surprise bills from headcount growth
- **No user friction** - onboard entire org without approval per seat
- **Massive savings** vs competitors: 500 users would cost $9k-30k/mo with Glean, $999/mo with Corporate Brain
- **Focus on value** - price on actual usage (data/query volume) not headcount
- **Service Provider tier** captures underserved MSP market

**Positioning Message:**
> "Stop paying per seat. Start paying for value. Most AI knowledge tools charge $20-60 per employee per month. Corporate Brain charges one flat monthly fee - add your entire organization without budget penalties."

### 3. Go-to-Market Strategy

**Primary Targets:**
1. **Regulated Industries** (Finance, Healthcare, Government) - Need sovereign deployment
2. **Service Providers/MSPs** - Need multi-tenant architecture
3. **Tech Companies** - Want transparent pricing, self-serve

**Secondary Targets:**
- European enterprises (GDPR/sovereignty concerns)
- Defense contractors (air-gapped requirements)
- Large enterprises with data residency requirements

## CONCLUSION

The enterprise AI knowledge management market is dominated by well-funded players (Glean: $2.2B, Moveworks: $2.1B) but has a clear gap: **no major competitor offers sovereign, multi-tenant deployment with transparent pricing**.

Corporate Brain's positioning as a "Sovereign Context Fabric" with multi-tenancy, transparent pricing, and open architecture represents a defensible market position targeting underserved segments (regulated industries, service providers, privacy-conscious enterprises).

**Key Success Factors:**
1. Deliver on sovereign deployment promise (air-gapped capability)
2. Maintain transparent pricing discipline as market differentiator
3. Build service provider channel for multi-tenant deployments
4. Emphasize data ownership and privacy vs. cloud-only competitors
